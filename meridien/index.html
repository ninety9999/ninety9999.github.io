01 — La Mécanique

Two hundred parts.
No batteries, ever.

Every Méridien movement is drawn, cut, bevelled and assembled under one roof on the Rhône. The going train you see here is not an illustration of our work — it is the work, rendered line for line from the drawings of Calibre M-72.

Wound by hand, it stores three days of running time and lets it go one beat at a time: 28,800 every hour, each one adjusted in six positions.
